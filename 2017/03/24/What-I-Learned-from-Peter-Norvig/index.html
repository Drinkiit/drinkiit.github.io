What I Learned from Peter Norvig

Posted on 2017-03-24 | In Computer Science |

This title is truly nothing more than a click bait. The thing is, I’ve just finished the course “Design of Computer Programs” by Peter Norvig on Udacity. This course is probably the best online course I have ever taken. I think even experienced software developer may be able to learn something from this course. Especially when you compare your solutions to that given by Peter and you will find that Peter’s solutions are far more elegant and succinct. But, besides the technical things, I would like to share two quotes by Peter in Lesson 6.

In lesson 6, Peter implemented a word game called Scrabble which he was playing with his friend Ken. With the help of this program, Peter could esaily cheat at the game. But he said,

The great American philosopher, Benjamin Parker once said, “With great power comes great responsibility.” We have a great power here to go through all the words in the dictionary and come up with all the best plays. But that wouldn’t be fair to my friend Ken, unless we had previously agreed that it was legal and fair to do so. I’m not going to do that. I got to resist that temptation. And throughout your career as an engineer, these types of temptations or these types of possibilities are going to come up. Having strong ethics is part of learning to be a good software engineer.

Programming could be daunting and frustrating sometimes. It’s not something that you know what to do at each stage. And I find that the following story will help a lot.

It may seem a little bit daunting, but there’s so much to do, and when I get that feeling, I remembered the book, Bird by Bird by Anne Lamott. In it, she relates her story in elementary school and there was a big book report due where she had to write up descriptions of multiple different birds. And she was behind and it was due soon, and she went to her father and complained, “How am I ever get done. I’m behind”, and her father just told her, “Bird by Bird.” “Just go and take the first bird, write up a report on that, and then take the next bird off the list and keep continuing until you’re done.” So Let’s go bird-by-bird and finish this up.
